Acute sore throat can be caused by viral pharyngitis or streptococcal pharyngitis.

Features suggesting strep pharyngitis include fever, tonsillar exudates, tender anterior cervical adenopathy, and absence of cough.

Airway compromise, stridor, severe dehydration, or inability to tolerate oral intake require emergency evaluation.

Patients with likely viral pharyngitis can usually receive supportive care at home.

Urgent care evaluation is reasonable for severe pain, dehydration risk, or worsening symptoms.
